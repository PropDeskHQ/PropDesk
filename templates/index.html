How We Score Player Props

When you pull up a prop market, PropDesk fetches the player's real game log and measures it against the line the books have set — not against a projected season total.

Recent performance carries more weight than season totals. A player's role, minutes, and form change over a season. What they've done in the last 5 games tells a more honest story than what they did in October.
We require real data before we'll rate anything. Thin samples get flagged or excluded automatically. If we don't have enough games to be confident, we say so — or we say nothing at all.
The line matters as much as the average. A player averaging 22 points doesn't make the Over on a 24.5 line a good bet. We measure actual hit rate at the specific line being offered today.
We weight recent games more than season totals. The model blends L5 (60%), L10 (25%), and full-season (15%) hit rates so that recent form dominates without completely ignoring longer-term patterns.
For MLB, the model also weights pitcher quality and batter profile. Season batting average, opposing pitcher ERA/WHIP, and lineup position all factor in — giving a true .300 hitter facing a 5.40 ERA starter more credit than a .240 hitter at the same line.
Confidence Tiers
🔒 ELITE
90%+ confidence. The player has hit this line in the overwhelming majority of recent games. L5, L10, and season hit rates all align.
✅ GREAT
75–89% confidence. Strong agreement across recent form, mid-term trend, and season data. The primary target range for most plays.
📊 GOOD
60–74% confidence. A clear positive lean at the posted line. Reasonable as a parlay leg — edge is present, but not dominant.
VALUE
45–59% confidence. Slight lean with limited sample or a tight historical spread. Use as supporting context — not as a standalone bet.
⬜ POOR
Below 45% confidence. The model finds little to no edge here. Hidden by default in the prop table — visible only when filtering is removed.
How We Grade Moneylines

We build a power rating for every team from their actual season data, then compare it to what the betting market is implying. When those two numbers disagree meaningfully, that's the edge.

Power ratings come from real season performance — win percentage, scoring margin, and recent form. All three matter. A team with a winning record that's been consistently outscored is rated very differently than one that's earned it.
Home advantage is factored in per sport. The edge of playing at home is real, measurable, and different for baseball, basketball, hockey, and football. We treat each sport separately.
In MLB, the starting pitcher is the single biggest variable. A great starter against a weak lineup can swing a game significantly. We pull confirmed starter data daily and weight it accordingly — it's never an afterthought.
In NBA, back-to-back fatigue is a real, measurable edge. Teams playing the second night of a back-to-back underperform at a rate the market doesn't always fully price in. We flag it every time.
We never show a strong edge on one factor alone. ELITE and GREAT ratings on a moneyline require multiple independent signals pointing the same direction. One decent stat isn't enough to call the market wrong.
How We Find Trends

Every trend on PropDesk is calculated from real completed games — not assumptions, not projections, not pattern-matching on tiny samples.

Player streaks — hit rates over a player's last 5, 10, or 15 games at their actual prop line. We only surface a streak when the hit rate is genuinely meaningful, not a coin flip with a round number attached.
Situational patterns (MLB) — real splits based on pitcher handedness, batting order position, home/away, rest days, and opponent quality. Every subset requires a minimum number of real qualifying games before we'll call it a system.
Market mispricing — when the book line sits significantly below a player's season output, or well above their recent form, we flag the gap. These are objective mismatches between the number and the data, not opinions.
Sharp money signals — when multiple sportsbooks move a line in the same direction since opening, it often reflects informed action. We track cross-book movement in real time and surface it when the signal is clear.
We'd rather show you three real trends than ten weak ones. Small samples are filtered out automatically — even if that means a lighter board on a given day.

What We Don't Do

We don't predict outcomes with certainty. Sports are unpredictable. Anyone who tells you otherwise is selling something. PropDesk shows you where the numbers point — you decide what to do with it.
✕
We don't force picks. Thin data, low sample sizes, and weak signals get filtered out automatically — even when that means fewer plays on a given day. We'd rather show you nothing than show you noise dressed up as confidence.
✕
We don't hide bad results. Every pick we surface is trackable. The record is what it is, unedited.
✕
We don't cherry-pick data. The model runs on the same rules for every player, every game, every sport. No manual overrides. No convenient omissions when the numbers aren't pretty.

We don't sell picks. Everything is computed in your browser from live data. No subscription tier unlocks better picks. No VIP channel. No upsell.
